Travel & On-Site Duties Policies

Practices and rules for booking travel and the expense reimbursement process at ATS.

General Definitions & Objectives

A] Objective

The ATS travel policy explains the rules & guidelines for booking travel and the expense reimbursement process. The purpose is to make employees aware of:

  • Travel & Out of Office Work Policies, Procedures, and Rules.
  • Promoting fairness in business travel expenses.
  • Ensuring maximum productivity without compromising Safety and Comfort.

B] Normal Place of Work

It is where the Employee normally delivers his Duties as per the requirement of the Roles and Responsibilities of the position for which he has been appointed/Assigned/Deputed.

C] "Site" OR out of Office Locations

Any Customer or Project location/place of Work other than and more than 60 km away from the Normal Place of Work.

D] Place of Deputation / Temporary Transfer

Is the place / Location / Site where an Employee will be Deputed/transferred to perform official Tasks that may last more than 2 months at a stretch.

E] What is Out of Office Duties (OOOD)?

Visiting/staying at Customer’s Work Sites away from Normal Place of Work for (< 2 weeks at a stretch):

  • Meetings
  • Sales Promotion
  • Project Execution
  • Services and Maintenance works

F] Travel for official Duties

Traveling to Project sites / Customer Locations for delivering the activities mentioned above.

G] On Duty Expenses

Expenses incurred for traveling, staying, and delivering expected tasks at Workplaces other than one's normal Place of Work.

Notes on Travel Eligibility:

  • As much as possible, all bookings shall be made through the company-appointed Travel Agency. When booked through agencies other than Company Agencies, Bills (receipts, invoices) are mandatory.
  • Expenses that may incurred towards any Travel plan changes/Mode changes, should be approved by the Reporting Managers/Sr Managers.
  • For continuous long travel of more than 8 hours, an out-of-pocket Travel Allowance of Rs 200 for every 8 hours of journey is payable. Not Applicable for Air Travel.

Important Notes & Conditions:

  • These allowance Rates are applicable for OOOD not exceeding 2 weeks at a stretch and NOT applicable for Employees who are on Temporary Transfer/Deputation etc.
  • Wherever the Company has Corporate Special rate tie-up with Hotels/Lodges, Employees are recommended to Book/Stay in such Hotels/Lodges only.
  • The No Bills allowances are applicable for Employees who prefer to make their own Boarding & Lodging arrangements. This is applicable for the entire trip and mixing "with Bills" and "without Bills" cannot be accepted.
  • As much as possible all the bookings are to be made through the Company appointed Travel Agency.
  • The Limits shown are net Charges including all Taxes.
  • If different Grade Employees travel together, Senior Employees make payments and claims covering the Junior Employee also.
  • If there are occasions of Customer Entertainment, Food Bills can be claimed at actual including the Food consumption of the Employee who is treating the Customer/Partner.
  • Expenses incurred towards Travel plan changes should be approved by Reporting Managers.
  • Management may consider suitable Rewards for Employees who are cost-conscious and save on the Eligible limits.
  • Employees can take Travel Advance as per approval of Assigning Manager to a maximum of ₹10,000 before travel with advance intimation to Accounts.
